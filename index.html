Bytes from My First Year as a Computer Science Student

May 14, 2024 by Sydonae E

I completed my first year as a Computer Science student a little over two weeks ago, and it was certainly an interesting experience. One major takeaway was the importance of making friends and helping others. I couldn’t have done it all on my own, especially when tackling tough Hackerrank challenges and labs. My friends were instrumental in getting through some of the most frustrating errors, and I also made new connections along the way. Helping others when I could was equally important—you never know when that favor might come back around.

Another important lesson was to study early. I fell victim to procrastination, and while it worked out in the first semester, the second semester was a completely different story. The stress was overwhelming, and although I survived, I realized how much easier it could’ve been with better time management. I also learned the hard way that staying focused in long lectures and attending tutorials is essential. It’s easy to fall behind, but playing catch-up only makes things worse. Next year, I'll definitely prioritize staying on top of things from the start.(Adapted from my Instagram)

Why Computer Science ?

July 11, 2022 by Sydonae E

When people ask me about my intended field of study, the conversation often takes a familiar turn: "What do you want to study?" "Medicine?" they reply, shocked when I say, "No, computer science or software engineering." Maybe it’s just the norm where I live for people to aspire to be doctors, and thankfully, we're slowly growing out of that mindset. I initially considered engineering in general, but computer science caught my eye after I enjoyed subjects like Math, Physics, and Information Technology. A visit to a robotics expo piqued my interest in coding, and after some shy Googling about careers in this field, I decided to explore it further.

Fast forward to Grade 11, where I was introduced to Pascal in my Information Technology class. If you’re starting out, I recommend trying EzyPascal to compile your code. Learning coding was significant for me because it brought satisfaction after each class, my teacher made it engaging, and I finally understood the concepts I had seen at the expo. Now, as I navigate my last two years of high school studying computer science, I realize I still don’t have a definitive answer to why I chose this path. Each day in this fascinating field brings me closer to discovering my passion for it, and I’m excited for the journey ahead! (Adapted from my Instagram)
